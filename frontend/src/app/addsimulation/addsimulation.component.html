The initialization part of the Water Tariff Dashboard involves configuring several essential components to define the socio-economic simulation environment.

How to proceed: First the primitives, then the population, then tarification, then the demand and finally, the launch.

  1. Tariff Module Initialization (Primitives) sets the general environment within which the pricing of the public water and wastewater services will be assessed.
  2. Population Module - Load the household-level data needed for microsimulation.
  3. Demand Module - Compute expected consumption.
  4. Tariff Module - Configure parameters for drinking water and (collective) sanitation.
  5. Launch - Run the simulation.

Demand Module

The demand equation in the dashboard of the water tariff tool utilizes a specific econometric model to estimate household water consumption based on various factors. The general functional form commonly referenced is based on a linear expenditure system.

Set the values of the response coefficients of the demand function.

Perception Parameter

The perception parameter is a key factor in the demand model utilized in the water tariff tool, playing an essential role in understanding how consumers perceive and respond to water pricing. This parameter essentially reflects the nature of consumer behavior in relation to how they interpret the costs associated with water consumption.

When the perception parameter approaches 1, consumers behave like Nordin consumers, focusing on the marginal price of water and adjusting their consumption based on the price of the last unit consumed; when it approaches 0, they behave like Taylor consumers, considering the average price of water and being less responsive to price changes.
