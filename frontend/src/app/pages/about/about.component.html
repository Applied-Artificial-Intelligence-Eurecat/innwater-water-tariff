What is the Water Tariff Dashboard?

The Water Tariff Dashboard is a core module of the InnWater Water Governance Platform, developed to support public authorities, utilities, researchers, and civil society in the evaluation and design of fair and sustainable water tariff systems. This digital tool enables comprehensive analysis, simulation, and comparison of tariff structures while offering intuitive visualizations and evidence-based metrics to strengthen informed decision-making in water management.

The dashboard allows users to create and run tariff simulations using different pricing schemes—such as Increasing Block Tariffs (IBT), flat-rate models, or targeted schemes—so that the socio-economic and environmental impacts of each strategy can be rigorously assessed. Users can configure tariff parameters, generate custom consumption-distribution scenarios, and evaluate how different household profiles and consumption behaviors respond to pricing signals.

A key feature of the dashboard is its ability to compare TBSE (Two-Part Tariff with Subsidized Essential Block Equivalent) models with IBT structures. The system provides interactive plots and performance visualizations, enabling users to explore consumption trends, affordability tiers, cost recovery dynamics, and incentive effects side-by-side. This helps reveal trade-offs between affordability protection, demand management, and financial sustainability.

Users can explore recognized indicators such as the Conventional Affordability Ratio (CAR) and Potential Affordability Ratio (PAR), alongside distribution-aware indices that better capture household vulnerability and affordability constraints across different socio-economic contexts.

Additionally, the Water Tariff Dashboard includes a serious-gaming mechanism that allows users to compare multiple simulations within competitive game rounds, fostering participatory policy exploration and collaborative learning. Stakeholders can define decision rules—such as weighting affordability versus incentive-based objectives—and observe which tariff designs score best under shared governance criteria.

To enhance usability, the dashboard integrates an AI assistant that provides real-time guidance and explanations of key indicators, helps interpret visual outputs, and supports the analysis workflow. Whether users are policy makers, technical experts, utility managers, or engaged citizens, the assistant facilitates understanding of complex metrics and strengthens capacity-building for water tariff governance.

Overall, the Water Tariff Dashboard empowers users to design, test, visualize, and compare water pricing strategies in a transparent, data-driven, and participatory environment—advancing equitable, efficient, and sustainable water governance in line with the InnWater vision.
